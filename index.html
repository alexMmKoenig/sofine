The most important financial innovation

that I have seen is the automatic teller machine. That really helps people.

Says Paul Volcker, former Chairman of the Federal Reserve and Chairman of the Economic Recovery Advisory Board under Obama from 2009 until 2011

Financial Innovation has brought about

a multitude of new products, such as subprime loans and niche credit programs for immigrants.

Thought Alan Greenspan, who served as Chairman of the Federal Reserve of the United States from 1987 to 2006.

Real financial innovation

- the drive to build new tools that increase clarity in valuation and promote capital formation for productive enterprises - was co-opted during the bubble years, perverted into schemes meant to obfuscate and create opacity in asset pricing.
